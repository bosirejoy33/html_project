Welcome

At the Kenya National Animal Orphanage, we are committed to the rescue, rehabilitation, and release of orphaned wildlife. Our dedicated team works tirelessly to provide a safe haven for these animals, ensuring they receive the care and support they need to thrive.

Our mission

The Kenya National Animal Orphanage is dedicated to the rescue and rehabilitation of orphaned wildlife.

In order to ensure what we are able to do at KNAO is possible , we rely on the support and generosity of individuals like you, linked here. to make a donation in helping keep our operations running smoothly.

We are also committed to educating the public about wildlife conservation and the importance of protecting our natural heritage.

Life at the Orphanage

Morning Feeding

8:00 AM — Our keepers begin the day with bottle-feeding orphaned calves and cubs. This ensures young animals get the nutrition they need for healthy growth.

Enrichment Activities

11:00 AM — Enrichment sessions include play, training, and introducing natural elements like branches or water pools to stimulate instincts.

“Enrichment is key to animal welfare in captivity.”
— KNAO Caretaker’s Handbook

Medical Check-ups

2:00 PM — Our veterinary team conducts health assessments, administers vaccines, and monitors progress of recovering animals.

Evening Calm

6:00 PM — As the sun sets, the animals return to their enclosures. Caretakers record daily observations for each orphan.

Did you know?

Each animal has a personalized care plan, recorded daily by our staff to track recovery and progress toward release.
